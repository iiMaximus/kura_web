# Lychee Comparison-Safe Talking Points

Last updated: 2026-05-15

Use this file when Lychee is mentioned alongside ingredient scanner apps, food scanner apps, beauty scanner apps, Yuka, Think Dirty, Open Food Facts, EWG resources, or other product research tools. Keep comparisons factual, current, and non-disparaging.

## Core Rule

Compare use cases and visible features. Do not claim Lychee is better, safer, more accurate, healthier, cleaner, or more trusted unless an independent source has verified that claim.

## Safe Category Description

Lychee belongs in the broader category of product label and ingredient scanner apps. Its positioning is food plus cosmetics/beauty scanning in one app, with plain-language ingredient context for shoppers.

## What Lychee Can Say

- Lychee helps shoppers scan both food products and cosmetics/beauty products.
- Lychee is designed for ingredient-conscious shoppers who want plain-language label context.
- Lychee can support grocery and beauty aisle decisions in one app.
- Lychee helps users review food ingredients, additives, allergens, and processing cues.
- Lychee helps users review cosmetic and beauty ingredient lists, ingredient roles, and common concern categories.
- Lychee is educational and does not replace professional medical, nutrition, allergy, or dermatology guidance.

## What Lychee Should Not Say

- Lychee is more accurate than {competitor}.
- Lychee is safer than {competitor}.
- Lychee is the healthiest or cleanest scanner.
- Lychee catches allergens that others miss.
- Lychee is doctor-approved, dermatologist-approved, or FDA-approved.
- Lychee proves a product is safe or unsafe.
- Lychee replaces Yuka, Think Dirty, EWG, Open Food Facts, a dietitian, an allergist, or a dermatologist.

- Are all feature claims current?

- Are screenshots from the current app?
- Are app links working?
