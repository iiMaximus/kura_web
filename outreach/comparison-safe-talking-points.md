# Kura Comparison-Safe Talking Points

Last updated: 2026-05-15

Use this file when Kura is mentioned alongside ingredient scanner apps, food scanner apps, beauty scanner apps, Yuka, Think Dirty, Open Food Facts, EWG resources, or other product research tools. Keep comparisons factual, current, and non-disparaging.

## Core Rule

Compare use cases and visible features. Do not claim Kura is better, safer, more accurate, healthier, cleaner, or more trusted unless an independent source has verified that claim.

## Safe Category Description

Kura belongs in the broader category of product label and ingredient scanner apps. Its positioning is food plus cosmetics/beauty scanning in one app, with plain-language ingredient context for shoppers.

## What Kura Can Say

- Kura helps shoppers scan both food products and cosmetics/beauty products.
- Kura is designed for ingredient-conscious shoppers who want plain-language label context.
- Kura can support grocery and beauty aisle decisions in one app.
- Kura helps users review food ingredients, additives, allergens, and processing cues.
- Kura helps users review cosmetic and beauty ingredient lists, ingredient roles, and common concern categories.
- Kura is educational and does not replace professional medical, nutrition, allergy, or dermatology guidance.

## What Kura Should Not Say

- Kura is more accurate than {competitor}.
- Kura is safer than {competitor}.
- Kura is the healthiest or cleanest scanner.
- Kura catches allergens that others miss.
- Kura is doctor-approved, dermatologist-approved, or FDA-approved.
- Kura proves a product is safe or unsafe.
- Kura replaces Yuka, Think Dirty, EWG, Open Food Facts, a dietitian, an allergist, or a dermatologist.

## Neutral Sentences For Roundups

- "Kura is worth considering if you want one scanner for both grocery labels and beauty ingredient lists."
- "Kura focuses on plain-language ingredient context across food, cosmetics, and beauty products."
- "Kura may fit shoppers who compare packaged foods, skincare, sunscreen, makeup, haircare, and personal care products."
- "As with any scanner app, users should check the current product label and treat app results as informational."

## Review Prompt For Editors

Before publishing a comparison, check:

- Are all feature claims current?
- Are competitor names spelled correctly?
- Are screenshots from the current app?
- Are app links working?
- Is Kura's food plus cosmetics/beauty positioning included?
- Are medical, allergy, nutrition, and dermatology caveats present?
- Is the article free of unsupported superlatives?
