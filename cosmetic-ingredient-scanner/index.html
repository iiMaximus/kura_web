Beauty Label Signals

What a cosmetic ingredient scanner can help you notice.

Ingredient function

Cosmetic labels include solvents, humectants, emollients, surfactants, preservatives, colorants, and fragrance components.

Concern categories

A flag can mean an ingredient deserves context for some users. It does not prove a finished product is harmful.

Use pattern

Leave-on versus rinse-off use, application area, frequency, and skin history can change how a label should be interpreted.

Lychee For Cosmetics

A practical scanner for long beauty ingredient lists.

Lychee is designed to make dense cosmetic labels easier to inspect before you buy or apply a product. It can explain ingredient roles and surface common sensitivity categories without treating every ingredient as good or bad in every context.

Lychee does not diagnose skin conditions, predict acne triggers, certify pregnancy safety, or replace advice from a dermatologist, allergist, pharmacist, or clinician.

Patch test and check labels

People with known allergies, irritated skin, or medical concerns should read the package carefully and use professional guidance when needed.
