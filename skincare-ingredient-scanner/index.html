Skincare Categories

Different product types need different caveats.

Cleansers

Rinse-off products may include surfactants, fragrance, preservatives, colorants, and pH adjusters.

Serums and creams

Leave-on products may include humectants, emollients, exfoliating acids, retinoid-like terms, and botanical extracts.

Sunscreen

UV filters and inactive ingredients can be hard to parse, and product instructions matter for real-world use.

How To Read Results

Ingredient concern is not the same as personal risk.

Skincare labels usually do not show exact concentration, manufacturing details, or how a product will interact with an individual skin history. Kura can explain what ingredients are commonly used for and why some may deserve attention.

For known allergies, broken or inflamed skin, pregnancy-related questions, medication interactions, or persistent reactions, use qualified medical or dermatology guidance.

Practical reminder

Patch testing and following product directions can matter as much as the ingredient list, especially for leave-on products.
