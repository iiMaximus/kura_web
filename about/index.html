What Kura Does

A scanner for labels that are too dense to read in a store aisle.

Kura is designed for packaged products where the ingredient list matters: snacks, drinks, cereals, sauces, protein products, skincare, makeup, haircare, deodorant, fragrance, and other beauty products.

The app can help explain ingredient names, additives, processing signals, common allergens, and cosmetic ingredient roles. It is meant to make labels easier to inspect, not to decide for you.

Important caveat

Packaging is the source of truth. Product formulas change, barcode databases can be incomplete, and personal health decisions should involve qualified medical, allergy, nutrition, or dermatology guidance when needed.

How To Read Kura

Use the scan as a starting point, then check the label.

A good product scanner should reduce confusion without pretending that every ingredient has one universal answer. Food risk can depend on diet, allergies, exposure, age, and medical context. Cosmetic risk can depend on concentration, leave-on versus rinse-off use, skin condition, and personal sensitivity.

Kura keeps those limits visible because a confident answer is not always the same as a correct answer.
